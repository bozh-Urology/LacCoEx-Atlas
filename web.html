Functional Enrichment Analysis — For each LRG, Pearson/Spearman correlations were computed against the full transcriptome across 33 TCGA cancer types, then integrated via random-effects meta-analysis (REML) to derive pan-cancer consensus estimates. Target genes were subsequently selected by two independent criteria: ① Top 200 by |meta_r| (strongest pan-cancer co-expression) and ② Top 200 by minimum I² (lowest inter-cancer heterogeneity, most consistent across cancers). GO (BP / MF / CC) and KEGG pathway enrichment analyses were performed on each gene set using clusterProfiler (BH-adjusted p < 0.05). Study Design & Statistical Methods

Overview

To comprehensively characterize the transcriptional co-expression relationships between lactylation-related genes (LRGs) and the broader RNA landscape, we systematically computed pairwise correlations between all 26 LRGs and protein-coding genes, lncRNAs, and miRNAs across all 33 TCGA cancer types using both Pearson and Spearman methods, then performed random-effects meta-analysis to derive pan-cancer consensus estimates.

Statistical Pipeline

Step 1 – Within-cancer correlation: Pearson r and Spearman ρ computed for each LRG–RNA pair within each of 33 TCGA cancer types.
Step 2 – Fisher's Z transformation: Correlation coefficients transformed to Z-scores for meta-analysis.
Step 3 – Random-effects meta-analysis: REML (Restricted Maximum Likelihood) estimation pooled Z-scores across 33 cancer types, accounting for inter-cancer heterogeneity.
Step 4 – Heterogeneity: I² statistic, Cochran's Q, and τ² reported per pair.
Step 5 – Multiple testing: Benjamini-Hochberg FDR correction within each RNA type.
Threshold: FDR < 0.05 and |meta_r| > 0.1
